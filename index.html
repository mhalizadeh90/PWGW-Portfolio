Mohammad Alizadeh

Lead Developer & Producer

Mohammad Alizadeh is a Senior Unity Developer and Producer based in Kraków, Poland, with over 10 years of experience in the game industry. As a co-founder of Team Lucky Dice, he has successfully managed remote teams across multiple countries, ensuring smooth collaboration and timely delivery of projects. Mohammad has participated in numerous game jams, demonstrating his dedication and ability to rapidly prototype ideas and turn them into playable experiences under tight deadlines.

He specializes in developing games using Unity, with several successful titles played by milions of international players like Cashman Casino. His work spans both digital and interactive projects, combining creativity with practical development skills. Mohammad’s experience in leading remote teams and coordinating cross-functional workflows has consistently resulted in high-quality, polished game releases.

Faezeh Khomeyrani

Game Designer & Level Designer

Faezeh Khomeyrani is an indie game designer and storyteller with a passion for creating narrative-driven experiences that connect with players on a personal level. She was drawn to game design by her love for video games and interactive storytelling. Since 2017, she has been part of the remote indie team Team Lucky Dice, developing experimental and narrative adventure games available on their Itch.io page. She also designed other creative game projects such as Dream Walk, a surreal walking simulator exploring memories and dreams in a 3D Unity environment, and Less Is Not More!, a card game raising awareness about privacy in the digital age through strategic gameplay. Her work often features branching paths and unexpected choices, aiming to leave a lasting impression through meaningful player engagement.

In addition to digital games, Faezeh has embraced tabletop design, finding joy in crafting non-digital experiences for face-to-face play. One of her notable projects is the board game Stairway To Heaven, where players embody souls choosing to become angels or demons, using cards and strategy to accumulate positive or negative points. Her semi-autobiographical game A Rejection Story won the A MAZE. Wings Diversity Award in 2022, highlighting her prolific output and focus on underrepresented voices in the industry. In 2023, she served as a jury member for the A MAZE. Berlin festival, further establishing her influence in the arthouse and indie game scene.

Michał Śledziński

Artist & Illustrator

Michał "Śledziu" Śledziński (ur. 1978 w Bydgoszczy) – rysownik, scenarzysta i twórca animacji. Absolwent Liceum Plastycznego w Bydgoszczy. Współtwórca i redaktor naczelny magazynów komiksowych Produkt i P-Lux oraz zina Azbest. Więcej informacji można znaleźć na Culture.pl.

Autor kultowych serii komiksów: Osiedle Swoboda, Fido i Mel, Strange Years, KDP, Czerwony Pingwin musi umrzeć!, Na szybko spisane i Wartości rodzinne. Pracował przy produkcji animacji, gier oraz reklam, współpracując m.in. z magazynami Secret Service, Świat Gier Komputerowych, Play, Pixel i PSX-Extreme.

W latach 2020–2023 pełnił funkcję Art Directora animowanej adaptacji klasycznej serii Kajko i Kokosz (Netflix) oraz był częścią zespołu reżyserskiego serialu. Reżyserował serial Kacperiada (na podstawie książek Grzegorza Kasdepke) i współtworzył zespół realizujący pełnometrażową animację Smok Diplodok.

Jest współtwórcą, animatorem i reżyserem serialu animowanego Ogar i Rogal oraz cinematiku do gry Warsaw. Aktualnie pracuje nad pełnometrażowymi animowanymi adaptacjami komiksów Tytus, Romek i A'Tomek (jako art director) oraz Osiedle Swoboda (jako scenarzysta i reżyser), a także nad kolejnymi odsłonami swoich serii komiksowych.

Wojciech Magdziak

Voice Over & Dubbing Director

Wojciech Magdziak – aktor, reżyser dubbingu, inżynier dźwięku i tłumacz. Pracuje w aktorstwie i reżyserii dźwięku. Do każdego projektu podchodzi z wyjątkową precyzją, starannie dbając o najmniejsze detale i estetykę. Znany z udziału w polskim dubbingu, m.in. w serialu Dragon Ball Z Kai (2023), gdzie użyczył głosu różnym postaciom, takim jak humanoidalny podwładny A, żółty, fioletowy i różowy podwładny, podwładny A oraz gwary podwładnych.

Jako fandubbingowy aktor i twórca, w 2013 roku dubbingował pijaka Farnhama i kowala Griswolda w projekcie Diablo. W 2020 roku powrócił do uniwersum Diablo, dubbingując wojownika (głos Paula Eidinga) oraz Na-Krula (Hellfire), a także pełnił funkcje reżysera, inżyniera dźwięku i tłumacza w studiu WiA Parker. Współtworzył polonizację gry Diablo 1: Hellfire, nadzorując nagrania, obsługę dźwięku i tłumaczenie dialogów.

Hamidreza Nikoofar

Composer & Sound Designer

Hamidreza Nikoofar is an Iranian composer, sound designer, and game designer with over 14 years of experience in video game audio. He began his career in Iran creating award-winning games as a designer, before shifting focus to music and sound, where he has excelled in blending orchestral, choral, ethnic, and electronic elements for immersive storytelling.

As Audio Director, Composer, and Sound Designer for Naser: Son of Man, a highly anticipated steampunk side-scroller, Nikoofar has led innovative sound efforts, composing a dynamic, genre-bending soundtrack and implementing interactive audio. He also has contributed to many other game projects like the viral mobile game Give It Up!.

His accolades include serving on the G.A.N.G. Awards committee (2020), Best Short Film wins for BPM: The Guilt (2020) and Tyrant (2017), and multiple nominations at the Tehran Game Festival for music, sound design, and game design. Beyond development, Nikoofar hosts the popular podcast Bonfire Conversations (formerly Wassup Conversations), interviewing Oscar winners and industry leaders in gaming, film, and tech, fostering connections at the intersection of interactive media.

Dr. Stanisław Jaśkowski

Historical Consultant

Dr. Stanisław Adam Jaśkowski is an assistant professor at the Department of Iranian Studies, Faculty of Oriental Studies, University of Warsaw. He holds an MA and PhD in Iranian Studies, focusing on Persian literature, history, and political thought. His doctoral work, “Historian, Social Critic, Prophet – Ahmad Kasravi and His Struggle”, explored reformist ideas in early 20th-century Iran.

A member of the Siglum Research Group for the Study of Manuscripts, Dr. Jaśkowski has taken part in international research programs at the Austrian Academy of Sciences and University of Tehran. His expertise includes Iranian cultural history, Persian palaeography, Qajar-era political thought, and Safavid diplomacy.

He is the author of Parcham – Journal of Ahmad Kasravi and His Followers and U źródeł współczesnego Iranu, and co-author of The Relations of the Polish-Lithuanian Commonwealth with Safavid Iran (2017). His forthcoming book, Letters in Silk Pouches (2025), continues his research on Persianate documentary culture.

Katarzyna Rodacka

Historian and Iran-study consultant

Katarzyna Rodacka is a writer and journalist specializing in the history and culture of Iran. Her debut book, Domy na piasku. Polacy w Iranie (1942–1945) (“Homes on Sand: Poles in Iran, 1942–1945”), was published by Znak Publishing House in April 2025. The book reconstructs the experiences of Polish refugees and soldiers who found temporary shelter in Iran during World War II.

A graduate in Iranian Studies from the Jagiellonian University, she divides her time between writing non-fiction and coordinating cultural events. Her award-winning reportage “Tłumacz umiera codziennie” (“The Translator Dies Every Day”), for which she received the Leopold Unger Scholarship for Young Journalists in 2021, explores the lives of Afghan and Iraqi interpreters who risked their lives working for international forces. She has also published essays and reports in major Polish outlets, often focusing on human rights and cross-cultural understanding.
