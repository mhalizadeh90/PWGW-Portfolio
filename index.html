Mohammad Alizadeh

Lead Developer & Producer

Mohammad Alizadeh is an Iranian software engineer and game developer who has made a name for himself in the indie gaming scene. Born and raised in Iran, he pursued a passion for technology and interactive entertainment, eventually relocating to Kraków, Poland, where he continues to blend his cultural roots with European influences. With a background in software engineering, Mohammad specializes in gameplay programming, creating unique mechanics that emphasize user experience and narrative depth. His work often explores themes of migration, adaptation, and cultural exchange, drawing from personal experiences to craft immersive digital worlds.

Over the past decade, Mohammad has developed several mobile games, including the successful title *Los Angeles Crimes*, which has garnered a dedicated following for its evolving mechanics and community-driven updates. As a solo developer, he maintains a Patreon to support his projects, allowing fans to contribute to faster development cycles. His narrative adventure games highlight innovative puzzle design and level creation, earning recognition in indie circles for their thoughtful integration of story and technology. Mohammad's commitment to indie development has positioned him as a bridge between Eastern and Western gaming traditions, inspiring aspiring developers through his accessible, self-taught approach.

Faezeh Khomeyrani

Game Designer & Level Designer

Faezeh Khomeyrani is an Iranian game designer known for her innovative blend of architecture and interactive storytelling. Originally trained in architecture at university, she transitioned into game design to pursue her lifelong love for video games, creating experimental and narrative-driven experiences. Faezeh's work often incorporates dark humor and unexpected paths, allowing players to explore complex themes through immersive worlds. Her remote collaborations with indie teams have resulted in a portfolio of games hosted on platforms like itch.io, where she experiments with non-linear narratives and player agency.

Among her notable achievements, Faezeh's interactive story about job searching earned the A MAZE. Wings Award in 2022, highlighting her skill in crafting semi-autobiographical tales that resonate globally. She has participated in numerous game jams, honing her ability to design under constraints, and has expanded into tabletop games, enjoying the tactile joy of non-digital concepts shared with friends. As a judge at A MAZE. Berlin in 2023, she contributes to the indie community, advocating for diverse voices in game design. Her resume, presented as an interactive experience, showcases her creative flair, making her a standout figure in narrative-focused gaming.

Michał Śledziński

Artist & Illustrator

Michał "Śledziu" Śledziński is a prominent Polish comic artist, scenarist, and animator renowned for his influential contributions to Polish pop culture. Born in 1978 in Bydgoszcz, he graduated from the Art High School there and began his career in the 1990s, co-founding magazines like Produkt, P-Lux, and the zine Azbest. Michał's style blends humor, social commentary, and urban realism, often drawing from his upbringing on Bydgoszcz's Szwederowo district, which inspired his iconic works. His transition into animation and games has expanded his reach, making him a versatile creator in visual storytelling.

Among his achievements, Michał's series Osiedle Swoboda (The Liberty Estate) revolutionized Polish comics, setting new standards with its raw depiction of everyday life and multiple editions. Other acclaimed works include Na Szybko Spisane, Wartości Rodzinne, and Czerwony Pingwin Musi Umrzeć!, earning him a dedicated fanbase. As Art Director for the Netflix adaptation of Kajko i Kokosz and director of series like Kacperiada, he has shaped Polish animation. Contributions to games, such as cinematics for Warsaw, and ongoing projects like animated adaptations of Tytus, Romek i A'Tomek highlight his enduring impact on comics and media.

Wojciech Magdziak

Voice Over & Dubbing Director

With a career rooted in theater and audio production, he approaches each endeavor with meticulous attention to detail, ensuring aesthetic excellence and emotional authenticity. Wojciech's work spans games and media, where he manages localization efforts to adapt content for Polish audiences, blending cultural nuance with technical skill. His passion for storytelling through sound has made him a go-to expert in post-production and actor direction.

Among his achievements, Wojciech led the Polish localization for the Diablo series, coordinating voice sessions that brought iconic characters to life for millions of players. Featured projects include directing trailers and in-game audio, showcasing his ability to enhance narratives with compelling performances. As a founder in related fields, he has contributed to educational and technological initiatives, fostering talent in voice acting. His cross-industry collaborations highlight his versatility, earning recognition for delivering high-quality dubbing that resonates across entertainment platforms.

Hamidreza Nikoofar

Composer & Sound Designer

Hamidreza Nikoofar is an Iranian composer, sound designer, and podcast host whose career spans games, film, and interactive media. Starting in Iran as a game designer, he created award-nominated titles before shifting to audio, where his expertise in dynamic soundtracks and immersive effects has earned international acclaim. Now based between Poland and Greece, Hamidreza blends orchestral, ethnic, and electronic elements to create genre-bending music that enhances storytelling. As the host of Bonfire Conversations (formerly Wassup Conversations), he interviews industry icons, fostering dialogues at the intersection of games, film, and technology.

His achievements include serving on the G.A.N.G. Awards committee in 2020, recognizing excellence in video game audio, and composing for viral mobile games like Give It Up! and the steampunk title Naser: Son of Man. Hamidreza's short films, such as BPM: The Guilt (Best Short Film at Super 9 Mobile Film Fest 2020) and Tyrant (Best Short Film at MINIKINO FILM WEEK 2017), demonstrate his directing and writing talents. Nominations at the Tehran Game Festival for music, sound design, and game design further underscore his versatility. As COO of Enlightened Robot Entertainment, he manages cross-country operations while implementing audio in engines like FMOD and Unity, solidifying his influence in entertainment.

Katarzyna Rodacka

Narrative Consultant

Katarzyna Rodacka is a Polish journalist and author with a deep focus on Iranian Studies, human rights, and social issues. A graduate of Iranian Studies at Jagiellonian University in Kraków, she combines academic rigor with storytelling to illuminate underreported narratives from the Middle East. Katarzyna's work often explores themes of migration, resilience, and cultural intersections, drawing from her travels and fieldwork in regions like Iran and Afghanistan. She balances her writing career with coordinating cultural events, bringing diverse perspectives to Polish audiences through magazines and online platforms.

Her notable achievements include winning the main prize of the Unger Scholarship for Young Journalists in 2021, honoring her impactful reporting on global injustices. Katarzyna's debut non-fiction book, Houses on the Sand: Poles in Iran (published by ZNAK in April 2025), delves into WWII-era stories of Polish refugees, earning praise for its empathetic and well-researched approach. Publications in outlets like Przekrój, Miesięcznik ZNAK, Nowa Europa Wschodnia, and Tygodnik Powszechny showcase her ability to tackle complex topics, from women's rights in Iran to social initiatives worldwide. As a multilingual writer fluent in Polish, English, French, and Persian, she continues to bridge cultural gaps through her insightful journalism.

Dr. Stanisław Jaśkowski

Historical Consultant

Stanisław Jaśkowski is a Polish scholar specializing in Iranian Studies, with a focus on Persian literature, history, and diplomacy. An Assistant Professor at the University of Warsaw's Department of Iranian Studies, he earned his MA in Cultural Studies (Iranian specialty) in 2010 and PhD in Persian Literature in 2015, with a dissertation on Ahmad Kasravi. Stanisław's research explores intellectual history from the Safavid to Pahlavi eras, incorporating auxiliary sciences like palaeography and diplomatics. His multilingual expertise in Polish, Persian, English, and others enables deep archival work across international institutions.

His achievements include leading the research grant "Minutes of the First Iranian Parliament (1906-1908): Translation and Analysis" (2020–2026) and co-authoring books like Relations of the Polish-Lithuanian Commonwealth with Safavid Iran (2017). Publications in journals such as Journal of Persianate Studies and Folia Orientalia address topics from Safavid seals to Qajar political thought. Scholarships in Tehran and Vienna, plus roles as an interpreter for Polish diplomatic missions, including during the 2021 Afghanistan evacuation, underscore his practical impact. Awards like the University of Warsaw Rector’s Prize (2021, 2023) recognize his contributions to Oriental Studies.
